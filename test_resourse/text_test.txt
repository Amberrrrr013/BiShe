Good morning, everyone.

I want to talk to you today about something we all share: a simple, humble seed. In the palm of your hand, it doesn't look like much. It's just a small, dry, quiet thing. It’s easy to overlook. But within that tiny shell is a story of impossible potential, a complete set of instructions for becoming a giant oak, a field of wheat, or a blooming flower. It holds sunlight, rain, roots, and branches, all folded up, waiting.

We are all like that seed. Every single one of us in this room carries within us a unique set of possibilities. Maybe yours is the blueprint for a brilliant idea, the melody for a beautiful song, the courage to start a difficult conversation, or the perseverance to solve a tough problem. But like the seed, our potential needs the right conditions to grow. It needs the soil of effort, the water of knowledge, and the sunlight of encouragement.

Too often, we look at others and see the mighty tree they’ve become—their accomplishments, their confidence, their fully realized success. And we look down at our own hands, at our own quiet seed, and we wonder if anything is there at all. We compare our beginning to someone else’s middle. We forget that the oak tree was once an acorn that refused to give up.

The journey from seed to tree is not a straight line. It’s a story of pushing through darkness, of reaching for light, of weathering storms. There will be dry spells that test your resolve. There will be winds that try to bend you. But with each small act of courage, with each bit of learning, with each day of showing up, you send down roots and stretch up a little taller.

So, I ask you today: What seed are you holding? What potential is waiting, quietly, inside you? Don't be afraid of its small beginning. Don't bury it in doubt. Plant it. Nourish it. Protect it. Give it what it needs to grow.

Because the world doesn't need more people wishing they were giant trees. The world needs more people brave enough to be seeds—to break open, to reach into the unknown, and to grow into what they were meant to be. Let’s start growing. Together.

Thank you.
